Theta Tau

Engineering leaders
for service, profession, and brotherhood

  • Tell Me More

Theta Tau is the nation's oldest, largest, and
foremost Fraternity for Engineers.

The purpose of Theta Tau is to develop and maintain a high standard of professional interest among its members, and to unite them in a strong bond of fraternal fellowship.

Founded in 1989 at Ohio University,
Rho Beta chapter continues to be the only professional engineering fraternity at Ohio University.

Rho Beta chapter remains one of the largest chapters of Theta Tau.

Leadership

With many available leadership positions, Theta Tau provides members opportunities to develop their leadership abilities and to be actively involved in making things happen.

Alumni

Members of Theta Tau remain members for life. We have a network of alumni working in many engineering fields who can provide advice, connections, and even jobs.

Scholarship

Rho Beta chapter encourages academic success. Members help each other with their scholarly pursuits. In addition, members are encouraged to actively participate in the professional society of their field.

Check out Rho Beta chapter

Service

We are known for our service to our college, university and the larger community. Our service projects create a unifying environment for learning and personal growth for our members.

Profession

We develop and nurture engineers with strong communication, problem-solving, collaboration, and leadership skills that we demonstrate in our profession, in our community, and in our lives.

Brotherhood

We forge lifelong bonds of fraternal friendship, a journey that develops and delivers a network of lasting personal and professional relationships. We foster an inviting, safe, and social environment in which our members become lifelong friends.

Engineering

We have a diverse range of engineers with members of all ages, backgrounds, and disciplines. Every ABET accredited engineering major at Ohio University is represented in Rho Beta chapter.
